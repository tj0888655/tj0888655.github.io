Key West

The southernmost point of the United States. Key West is a paradise for sun-seekers, water-lovers, history buffs, and adventure seekers. You can enjoy the stunning beaches, snorkel in the coral reefs, visit the Ernest Hemingway Home and Museum, or take a sunset cruise on a catamaran. Whether you want to relax or explore, Key West has something for everyone. Don't miss this chance to experience the beauty and charm of this unique island.

Islamorada

Islamorada is a paradise for anglers, divers, boaters, and nature lovers. You can enjoy world-class fishing, snorkeling, kayaking, and wildlife watching in the clear blue waters of the Atlantic Ocean and the Florida Bay. You can also explore the rich history and culture of Islamorada, from its Native American roots to its pirate legends to its modern art and cuisine. Whether you want to relax on the beach, have an adventure on the water, or experience the local flavor, Islamorada has something for everyone. Don't miss this chance to discover one of the most beautiful and diverse destinations in the world.

Dry Tortugas

A cluster of seven islands located 70 miles west of Key West. Dry Tortugas is home to stunning coral reefs, diverse marine life, historic shipwrecks, and the majestic Fort Jefferson, the largest brick structure in the Western Hemisphere. You can explore the park by snorkeling, diving, kayaking, fishing, camping, or simply relaxing on the white sandy beaches.
